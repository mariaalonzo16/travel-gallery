Discover the best restaurants and attractions in Madrid, Barcelona, Valencia and Palma de Mallorca. Learn travel hacks and avoid tourist traps with our list of best places to visit and eat in Spain.

With endless cultural festivals, world-class beaches, and crazy nightlife, Spain is impressive 365 days a year. You could spend years exploring Spain. From Madrid to Barcelona to Valencia to Palma de Mallorca, it’s such a vibrant country.

Whether you hit the streets of Calle Gran Via, wander the beaches of Cala Mallorca, or worship the sun in Valldemossa, Spain is guaranteed to impress you any day of the week.

What To Expect

Language:

The official language of Spain is Spanish.

Currency:

The currency in Spain is the Euro.

Credit Cards and ATMs:

You won’t have any problems finding ATMs around Spain unless you go really off grid. If you’re spending time in an isolated area, it’s best to keep enough cash on you for the basics. Mastercard and Visa are accepted at most machines as well as restaurants and shops.

Plugs:

The plugs in Spain are type C and F. The standard voltage is 230 V, and the standard frequency is 50Hz. It is highly recommended to buy a universal adapter (make sure it has surge protection) and using a converter for hairdryers and hot tools.

Safety:

Spain is one of the safest European countries. While there is a low crime rate, you’ll need to be aware of pickpockets in the metros and in crowded areas that are popular with tourists.
